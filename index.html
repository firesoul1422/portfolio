Hybrid ASR-T5 for French Speech Recognition

2025 – 2026
Dual-encoder architecture for production systems · Praxy.ai

A dual-encoder speech-recognition architecture that pairs a FastConformer acoustic encoder with T5's full encoder-decoder through a lightweight adapter — using the language model's encoder for linguistic enrichment before generation, unlike encoder-only (Whisper) or decoder-only designs.

83.3% win rate over the production baseline
ASRT5FastConformerLoRANeMoPyTorch
Problem

Production ASR must stay accurate and robust across very different domains. Existing hybrids use only a language model's encoder (Whisper) or only its decoder — leaving linguistic capacity on the table.

Approach

Designed a dual-encoder hybrid: a FastConformer acoustic encoder feeds T5's complete encoder-decoder via a ~4M-parameter adapter, with LoRA adaptation and a dedicated silence-handling strategy to prevent hallucinations.

Result

An 83.3% win rate (15/18 categories) over the production NeMo baseline trained on identical data, 3.9% validation WER (competitive with Canary and Voxtral), and an 18.6% relative WER reduction in the medical domain — with strong zero-shot transfer (22.78% WER with no medical training data). Being prepared for production deployment.

Baseer — Historical Place Detection

Aug – Dec 2024
Detecting historical places and generating detailed explanations

A vision model on a modified CMT architecture that detects historical places, paired with an NLP RAG system (Sambalingo) and a chatbot interface that explains each detected site in detail.

97% — beats original CMT at 0.01% of its parameters
Computer VisionCMTRAGChatbot
Problem

Recognizing a historical site and explaining it well needs vision and language working together.

Approach

Developed Baseer on a modified CMT architecture for detection, then built an NLP RAG system (Sambalingo) with a chatbot interface to provide detailed explanations of detected sites.

Result

Reached 97% accuracy — a higher score than the original CMT architecture while using just 0.01% of its parameters, showing that a far smaller model can beat the full one.
